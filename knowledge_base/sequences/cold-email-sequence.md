# Cold Email Sequence

A 5-step outbound email cadence designed for B2B cold outreach. Each step
has a distinct purpose, tone, and structure. The sequence builds pressure
gradually while providing genuine value at every touch.

## Sequence Overview

| Step | Day | Purpose | Tone |
|------|-----|---------|------|
| 1 | 0 | PVC opener — earn attention | Direct, personalized |
| 2 | 3 | Value-add with content | Helpful, generous |
| 3 | 7 | New angle / different pain | Curious, fresh |
| 4 | 14 | Social proof / case study | Credible, specific |
| 5 | 21 | Breakup with value | Honest, low-pressure |

## Step 1: PVC Opener (Day 0)

**Purpose:** Earn the right to their attention. Show you've done homework.
Make them think "this person actually looked at what I'm doing."

**Structure:**
- Permission line (reference their world)
- Value line (connect to what they care about)
- CTA (low-friction question)

**Subject Line Pattern:** Lowercase, 2-4 words, looks human.
Examples: "quick thought on [topic]", "[first name] — [specific thing]",
"[their company] + [your value]"

**Tone:** Direct, specific, human. No fluff. Under 75 words.

**Example:**
> Saw you just brought on 3 new AEs — scaling outbound post-Series B
> is a different game than founder-led sales.
>
> We help teams like yours build repeatable pipeline without adding
> headcount faster than revenue.
>
> Worth a quick look?

## Step 2: Value-Add (Day 3)

**Purpose:** Deliver value with no ask. Share something relevant to their
situation — a resource, insight, benchmark, or framework.

**Structure:**
- Brief callback ("Sent a note Tuesday — no worries if it got buried")
- Value payload (link, insight, or data point)
- Soft CTA or none at all

**Subject Line Pattern:** Re: original thread (keeps the thread going).
Or: "[relevant topic] — thought you'd find this useful"

**Tone:** Generous, helpful. You're a peer sharing knowledge, not a
rep pushing a meeting.

**Example:**
> Came across this breakdown of how [similar company] structured their
> outbound engine post-Series B. Thought it might be relevant given
> where you're at.
>
> [link]
>
> No pitch — just thought it was useful.

## Step 3: New Angle (Day 7)

**Purpose:** Approach from a completely different pain point or angle.
If Step 1 didn't resonate, this might hit a different nerve.

**Structure:**
- New opening (don't reference previous emails)
- Different pain or use case
- Fresh CTA

**Subject Line Pattern:** New subject line — treat it like a fresh email.
Examples: "[different pain point]?", "question about [different topic]"

**Tone:** Curious, conversational. Ask a genuine question about their
business.

**Example:**
> Curious — how's your team handling personalization at scale right now?
> Most teams we talk to are stuck choosing between quality and volume.
>
> We've been helping [type of company] do both without the tradeoff.
> Worth exploring?

## Step 4: Social Proof (Day 14)

**Purpose:** Let someone else make your case. Share a specific result
from a similar company. Specificity is everything here.

**Structure:**
- Brief context
- Specific proof point (numbers, outcomes, timeline)
- Bridge to their situation
- CTA

**Subject Line Pattern:** "[similar company] did [specific result]",
"how [company type] solved [pain]"

**Tone:** Credible, specific. Numbers over adjectives. Outcomes over
features.

**Example:**
> [Similar company] was in a similar spot — 4 AEs, Series B, trying
> to build pipeline without hiring an army of SDRs.
>
> In 60 days they went from 12 qualified meetings/month to 38, with
> the same team.
>
> Given where [their company] is headed, might be worth comparing
> approaches. Open to a quick chat?

## Step 5: Breakup with Value (Day 21)

**Purpose:** Close the loop with dignity. Don't guilt-trip. Leave the
door open and provide one last piece of value.

**Structure:**
- Honest acknowledgment ("Looks like the timing isn't right")
- One final value piece
- Open door, no pressure

**Subject Line Pattern:** "closing the loop", "last one from me",
"one last thought"

**Tone:** Honest, respectful, low-pressure. No passive-aggression.

**Example:**
> Looks like timing isn't right — totally get it.
>
> Last thing I'll share: [relevant resource or insight]. Useful
> regardless of whether we ever chat.
>
> If this becomes a priority down the road, I'm easy to find.
> Cheers, [name]

## Rules for the Full Sequence
- Never send more than one email per step
- If they reply at any point, exit the sequence and have a human convo
- Personalization should be real — if you can't personalize, don't fake it
- Each email should stand alone (they may not have read the others)
- Total sequence duration: 21 days. After that, move to a nurture cadence
- Measure: open rates on steps 1-3, reply rates on steps 3-5
