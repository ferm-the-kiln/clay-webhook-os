# Displacement Playbook

Replacing an incumbent vendor is the hardest sale. The prospect already has
something that "works." Your job is to make the status quo uncomfortable
enough that change feels easier than staying.

Three scenarios, three distinct strategies.

## Scenario 1: Unhappy Customer

They're already frustrated with their current vendor. This is your best
opportunity — but don't waste it by piling on.

**Strategy: Validate and Bridge**

1. **Validate frustration** — "That pain is real. We hear it a lot."
2. **Don't bash the incumbent** — They chose it. Bashing it feels like
   bashing their judgment.
3. **Offer a migration path** — Make switching feel low-risk
4. **Quantify the cost of staying** — Frame in terms they care about
   (hours wasted, pipeline lost, deals slipped)

**Messaging Pattern:**
"Sounds like [pain they described] is costing your team real time. Most
teams in your position want to know two things: how hard is migration,
and how fast do results change. Happy to walk through both."

**Key Signal Data:** Support complaints, G2 reviews mentioning churn,
job postings for roles that shouldn't be needed if tool worked.

## Scenario 2: Unaware Customer

They don't know they have a problem. Their current tool is "fine" because
they haven't seen what "great" looks like.

**Strategy: Introduce New Criteria**

1. **Don't lead with your product** — Lead with the gap they don't know
   they have
2. **Ask questions that reveal the gap** — "How do you handle X today?"
   (when you know they probably don't)
3. **Share peer insights** — "What we're seeing with similar teams is..."
4. **Plant the evaluation seed** — "Might be worth pressure-testing your
   current setup against these criteria"

**Messaging Pattern:**
"Most [role] at [company type] assume [current approach] is the standard.
The teams pulling ahead are doing [better approach] instead. Curious how
you're thinking about it."

**Key Signal Data:** Company growth signals that outpace their current
tooling, industry shifts they may not have adapted to.

## Scenario 3: Just-Renewed Customer

They recently renewed with the incumbent. They're locked in for 12-18
months. This is a long game.

**Strategy: Seed and Nurture**

1. **Don't push for a deal now** — You'll lose credibility
2. **Build the relationship** — Be the expert they trust, not the vendor
   who's selling
3. **Share valuable content** — Insights, benchmarks, trends
4. **Set a calendar reminder** — Re-engage 90 days before renewal
5. **Create internal champions** — Find the person who's frustrated but
   can't act yet

**Messaging Pattern:**
"No pitch here — I know you just renewed with [category]. Just wanted
to share [relevant insight] that might be useful regardless. Happy to
be a resource whenever it makes sense."

**Timeline:** Touch base every 6-8 weeks with genuine value. Increase
frequency 90 days before renewal window opens.

## Switching Cost Calculation Framework

Help prospects quantify the real cost of NOT switching. Frame switching
costs against staying costs.

### Cost of Staying (Annual)
- Hours wasted on workarounds: __ hrs/week x __ people x hourly rate
- Revenue lost from inefficiency: __ deals/quarter x avg deal size
- Opportunity cost: what could team do with recovered time?
- Hidden costs: extra tools needed to fill gaps, manual processes

### Cost of Switching (One-Time)
- Migration time: typically 2-4 weeks for data, 4-8 weeks for adoption
- Learning curve: 2-4 weeks to full productivity
- Temporary productivity dip: factor 20-30% reduction for 30 days

### The Math
If cost of staying annually > 2x cost of switching, the case is clear.
Frame it: "You'll recover the switching cost in [X] months."

## Migration Risk Mitigation Messaging

The #1 objection to switching is risk. Address it directly.

**Parallel Run:** "Run both systems for 30 days. Compare results
side-by-side. Only cut over when you're confident."

**Phased Migration:** "Start with one team or one use case. Expand
once you see results. No big-bang required."

**Data Portability:** "Your data is yours. We'll help you export
everything if it doesn't work out. No lock-in."

**Rollback Plan:** "If something breaks, here's exactly how we
roll back. We document this before we start."

## Anti-Patterns
- Pushing for a deal when they just renewed (burns trust)
- Leading with a competitive comparison chart (commoditizes you)
- Offering deep discounts to displace (trains them to negotiate hard)
- Ignoring the champion who chose the incumbent (they'll block you)
- Underestimating how hard switching actually is for the buyer
