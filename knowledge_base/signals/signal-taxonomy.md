# Signal Taxonomy

Buying signals are observable events that indicate a company is more likely to
buy in the near term. The right signal, at the right time, to the right person
turns cold outbound into warm outbound.

## Signal Categories

### 1. Funding Round
**What it means:** New capital and a mandate to deploy it. Post-raise teams
must hit growth milestones tied to the raise.
**Who to contact:** CEO (seed/A), CRO or VP Sales (B/C), functional VP.
**Urgency window:** 14-45 days. After 60 days the plan is locked.
**Why it matters:** Money plus pressure equals buying velocity.

### 2. Hiring Surge
**What it means:** Active job postings signal investment in a function. Hiring
SDRs means outbound investment. Hiring engineers means product buildout.
**Who to contact:** The hiring manager or department VP.
**Urgency window:** 7-30 days from posting.
**Why it matters:** Every hire is a problem statement. Your product might
replace or accelerate the headcount.

### 3. Tech Stack Change
**What it means:** Company adopted, dropped, or switched a tool. Visible via
technographic data, job postings mentioning tools, or G2 reviews.
**Who to contact:** RevOps, GTM Ops, or the functional owner of the category.
**Urgency window:** 14-60 days. Integration periods create openness.
**Why it matters:** Stack changes mean active workflow re-evaluation.
Adjacent categories get pulled into the review.

### 4. Leadership Change
**What it means:** New executive hire (VP+). New leaders bring new playbooks,
new vendor relationships, and a mandate to show results in 90 days.
**Who to contact:** The new hire directly — they're open to introductions.
**Urgency window:** 7-30 days from start date.
**Why it matters:** New leaders have budget authority and no loyalty to
existing vendors yet.

### 5. Product Launch
**What it means:** Company shipped a new product, feature, or entered a new
market. They need to generate demand for it.
**Who to contact:** VP Marketing, Product Marketing, or CRO.
**Urgency window:** 7-21 days. Launch energy fades quickly.
**Why it matters:** New products need pipeline. If your tool helps generate
demand or reach new markets, the timing is perfect.

### 6. Geographic / Market Expansion
**What it means:** New office, region, or market segment. Visible via press
releases, job postings in new locations, or regulatory filings.
**Who to contact:** VP Sales (territory), VP Marketing (segment), or
Head of International.
**Urgency window:** 14-45 days.
**Why it matters:** Expansion means net-new pipeline needs. Existing systems
often don't cover the new territory.

### 7. Partnership Announcement
**What it means:** Strategic partnership, integration, or channel deal.
**Who to contact:** VP Partnerships, VP Sales, or CRO.
**Urgency window:** 14-30 days. Adjacent needs emerge (enablement,
co-marketing, data sharing).
**Why it matters:** Partnerships open adjacent budget conversations.

### 8. Acquisition
**What it means:** M&A triggers system consolidation, team restructuring,
and new budget allocation.
**Who to contact:** CRO or VP of the function being consolidated.
**Urgency window:** 30-90 days. Integration is slow but budgets unlock early.
**Why it matters:** Two stacks become one. Whoever gets in early shapes the
consolidated stack.

## Using Signals in Outbound

1. **Never reference a signal without connecting it to their pain.** "Congrats
   on the raise" is lazy. "Scaling outbound post-Series B without 3x-ing
   headcount is the hard part" earns attention.
2. **Stack signals for higher confidence.** Funding + hiring SDRs + new CRM =
   actively building a GTM engine. Prioritize accordingly.
3. **Respect the window.** A 90-day-old funding round is not a signal anymore.
   Recency is what makes signals work.
