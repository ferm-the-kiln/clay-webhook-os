# Signal Taxonomy

Buying signals are observable events that indicate a company is more likely to
buy in the near term. The right signal, at the right time, to the right person
turns cold outbound into warm outbound.

## Signal Categories

### 1. Funding Round

**What it means:** Company has new capital and a mandate to deploy it. Post-raise
teams are under pressure to hit growth milestones tied to the raise.

**Who to contact:** CEO (seed/A), CRO or VP Sales (B/C), VP of the function
your product serves.

**Urgency window:** 14-45 days. Budgets get allocated fast post-close. After 60
days the plan is locked.

**Why it matters:** Money plus pressure equals buying velocity. They need to
spend to grow, and they need to grow to justify the valuation.

### 2. Hiring Surge

**What it means:** Active job postings signal investment in a function. Hiring
SDRs means outbound investment. Hiring engineers means product buildout.

**Who to contact:** The hiring manager listed on the role. If no name, the
department VP.

**Urgency window:** 7-30 days from posting. The pain is active — they haven't
solved it with headcount yet.

**Why it matters:** Every hire is a problem statement. "We're hiring 5 SDRs"
means "we need more pipeline." Your product might replace or accelerate that.

### 3. Tech Stack Change

**What it means:** Company adopted, dropped, or switched a tool in their stack.
Visible via technographic data (e.g., new tracking scripts, job postings
mentioning tools, G2 reviews).

**Who to contact:** RevOps, GTM Ops, or the functional owner of the tool
category.

**Urgency window:** 14-60 days. Integration and evaluation periods create
openness to adjacent tools.

**Why it matters:** Stack changes mean the team is actively re-evaluating
workflows. Adjacent categories get pulled into the review.

### 4. Leadership Change

**What it means:** New executive hire (VP+). New leaders bring new playbooks,
new vendor relationships, and a mandate to show results in 90 days.

**Who to contact:** The new hire directly. They're building relationships and
open to introductions.

**Urgency window:** 7-30 days from start date. The "learning and evaluating"
window closes fast.

**Why it matters:** New leaders have budget authority and motivation to make
early wins. They haven't inherited loyalty to existing vendors yet.

### 5. Product Launch

**What it means:** Company shipped a new product, feature, or entered a new
market. They need to generate demand for it.

**Who to contact:** VP Marketing, Product Marketing, or CRO depending on the
GTM motion.

**Urgency window:** 7-21 days. Launch energy fades quickly. They're thinking
about distribution right now.

**Why it matters:** New products need pipeline. If your tool helps generate
demand, distribute content, or reach new markets — the timing is perfect.

### 6. Geographic / Market Expansion

**What it means:** New office, new region, new market segment. Visible via press
releases, job postings in new locations, or regulatory filings.

**Who to contact:** VP Sales (new territory), VP Marketing (new segment), or
Head of International.

**Urgency window:** 14-45 days. Expansion plans move fast once announced.

**Why it matters:** Expansion means net-new pipeline needs. Existing systems
often don't cover the new territory or segment.

### 7. Partnership Announcement

**What it means:** Strategic partnership, integration, or channel deal. Signals
the company is investing in ecosystem growth.

**Who to contact:** VP Partnerships, VP Sales, or CRO.

**Urgency window:** 14-30 days. The partnership creates adjacent needs
(enablement, co-marketing, data sharing).

**Why it matters:** Partnerships open adjacent budget conversations. If your
product complements the partnership, you ride the momentum.

### 8. Acquisition

**What it means:** Company acquired or was acquired. M&A triggers system
consolidation, team restructuring, and new budget allocation.

**Who to contact:** CRO or VP of the function being consolidated. Post-merger
integration leads.

**Urgency window:** 30-90 days. M&A integration is slow but budgets unlock
early.

**Why it matters:** Acquisitions force tool re-evaluation. Two stacks become
one. Whoever gets in early shapes the consolidated stack.

## Using Signals in Outbound

1. **Never reference a signal without connecting it to their pain.** "Congrats
   on the raise" is lazy. "Scaling outbound post-Series B without 3x-ing
   headcount is the hard part" earns attention.

2. **Stack signals for higher confidence.** Funding + hiring SDRs + new CRM =
   this account is actively building a GTM engine. Prioritize accordingly.

3. **Respect the window.** A 90-day-old funding round is not a signal anymore.
   It's old news. Recency is what makes signals work.
