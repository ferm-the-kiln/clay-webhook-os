# Signal-Based Opener Patterns

Every opener earns Permission by referencing the signal specifically, then
bridges to Value. These are structural patterns — swap in the real details
from the prospect's signal data.

## Principles

- The opener IS the Permission line from PVC. It must prove you did homework.
- Reference the signal with enough specificity that it can't be copy-pasted
  to another prospect.
- Never congratulate without connecting to a challenge.
- One signal per opener. Don't cram multiple signals into the first line.

## Funding Round

**Pattern A — The Scale Problem**
"Saw {company} closed {round_size} {round_type} — scaling {function} post-raise
without burning through that runway in 12 months is the real puzzle."

**Pattern B — The Mandate Shift**
"{round_type} changes the game — suddenly the board wants repeatable pipeline,
not founder-led deals. That shift usually breaks a few things."

## Hiring Surge

**Pattern A — The Job Posting Tell**
"Noticed {company} is hiring {n} {role_title}s — that usually means
{inferred_pain} is the priority this quarter."

**Pattern B — The Ramp Problem**
"Hiring {role_title}s is the easy part. Getting them productive in under 60
days without a documented playbook — that's where it gets expensive."

**Pattern C — The Build vs. Buy**
"Saw the {role_title} posting. Some teams hire for that; others solve it with
tooling first and hire on top. Depends on the timeline."

## Tech Stack Change

**Pattern A — The Integration Window**
"Saw {company} moved to {new_tool} — the first 90 days on a new {category}
usually surfaces gaps in {adjacent_function}."

**Pattern B — The Stack Question**
"Teams that switch {category} tools usually end up re-evaluating
{adjacent_category} too. Curious if that's on your radar."

## Leadership Change

**Pattern A — The New Playbook**
"Saw you joined {company} as {title} — new leaders usually have 90 days to
show the board something different. Curious what you're rethinking."

**Pattern B — The Vendor Reset**
"New {title} at {company} — in my experience, the first thing that gets
re-evaluated is the {category} stack. Fair assumption?"

**Pattern C — The Quick Win**
"First 60 days in a new role, most {title}s look for one quick win to build
credibility. Usually it's {common_quick_win}."

## Product Launch

**Pattern A — The Distribution Problem**
"Saw {company} just launched {product}. Building it is one thing — making sure
{target_audience} actually hears about it is the harder half."

**Pattern B — The Pipeline Gap**
"New product means new pipeline needs. Curious how {company} is thinking about
demand gen for {product} beyond the launch PR cycle."

## Geographic / Market Expansion

**Pattern A — The Cold Start**
"Saw {company} is expanding into {market/region}. Starting pipeline from zero
in a new market without the brand recognition is a different game."

**Pattern B — The Playbook Gap**
"What works in {current_market} rarely copy-pastes to {new_market}. The
outbound motion usually needs a full rethink."

## Partnership Announcement

**Pattern A — The Ecosystem Play**
"Saw the {partner} partnership — that opens a lot of doors but also means
{company} needs a co-selling motion that probably doesn't exist yet."

**Pattern B — The Enablement Gap**
"Partnerships get announced in a press release but actually work when the
sales team knows how to position the joint story. Curious how that's going."

## Acquisition

**Pattern A — The Consolidation Window**
"Post-acquisition, the first thing that gets messy is the GTM stack — two
CRMs, two outbound tools, two data vendors. That consolidation window is
where most teams waste 6 months."

**Pattern B — The Integration Play**
"Saw {company} acquired {target}. Merging two sales teams' workflows is
usually harder than merging the products."

## Usage Rules

1. **Pick the pattern that matches your data depth.** If you only know the
   signal event, use Pattern A. If you have richer context, use B or C.
2. **Fill in specifics or don't send.** A pattern with {placeholders} still
   visible is worse than no email at all.
3. **Follow with a PVC Value line.** The opener earns permission — the next
   sentence must deliver value by connecting your product to the inferred pain.
4. **One opener per email.** Save additional signals for follow-up touches.
