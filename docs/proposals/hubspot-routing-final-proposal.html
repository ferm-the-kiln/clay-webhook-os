HubSpot Parent-Child Routing

LinkedIn-first contact routing for parent-child company structures
Proposal — March 2026

The Problem

HubSpot has no parent-child awareness. When a subsidiary employee's email uses the parent domain (@nfl.com), they get assigned to the parent record — even when their LinkedIn clearly shows they work at a child company.

Today

  • Email domain is the only signal
  • @nfl.com = NFL, always
  • Chiefs employees go to the NFL rep
→

With This

  • LinkedIn Company URL is the routing signal
  • HubSpot parent-child records = source of truth
  • Every decision has a clear reason

Every Scenario, One Table

Clay enriches the contact's LinkedIn profile and compares the Company URL against parent and child records in HubSpot. Every contact lands in one of four buckets:

LinkedIn URLKnown Child?Decision
Already on correct child — Skip — already correct
≠ parent (e.g. Chiefs) Yes Auto-associate to Chiefs
≠ parent (unknown company) No Flag for rep — possible new subsidiary
= parent (NFL) — Keep on NFL
= parent + title mentions child — Flag for rep — title suggests subsidiary
(missing) — Flag for rep — no LinkedIn data
When in doubt, we flag for a human. This doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to be better than no parent-child awareness at all.

Rollout

1

Discovery

Claude analyzes existing HubSpot contacts, identifies parent-child relationships. Team reviews a spreadsheet and approves before anything changes. Claude's role ends here.

2

Automated Routing

Build the routing logic in Clay. Clay already links companies to HubSpot IDs — we just make sure it picks the right company ID.

3

Dedup Safety Net

Check before creating, check after creating, scan weekly. Duplicates won't survive.

4

Scale

Roll out to all parent companies once the pilot validates. Add monitoring dashboard.
